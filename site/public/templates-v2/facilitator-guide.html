Timing pro tip — sections 4–5 (15:00–35:00) are where you spend the most oxygen. Diagnosis sets up the moves. If you're light on diagnosis time, the moves still land because they're pre-drafted. If you're light on moves time, the diagnosis lands but the action plan needs more polish in review. Never skip the First Move — that's where buy-in calcifies.

Red Flags + Recovery

Can't articulate what they want
"What's one thing that would make this feel worth doing?"
Strips away abstraction. Forces a concrete anchor.
Pushes back on the diagnosis
"Tell me more about that."
Adjust if they're right. Don't defend the read. Being wrong fast = trust earned.
Wants execution during the call
"That's a build, not a call. Let's map what that build looks like."
Then move to Section 5 / the moves.
In actual crisis (funding, layoffs, etc.)
"Before strategy — are you okay?"
Shift from diagnosis to support. Sometimes the workshop needs to become a different conversation.
Gives the polished investor pitch answer
"That sounds like the version you tell investors. What's the real version?"
Cuts to the actual question.
Session isn't landing by minute 15
Trigger Recalibration check early.
Better to reset than push through a session that's off.
M + J disagree in real time
"There's a tension here worth exploring."
Make it visible. Don't smooth it over. Disagreement is signal — name it.
00
0:00–2:00 · Both

Introductions — set the pace.

Fast, warm, no fluff. You model the cadence for the entire session. If the intro drags, everything drags.

The Flow

Megha (30 sec) — name, what you do, one thing.
Jake (30 sec) — same structure.
Client (60 sec) — start the micro-timer. Let them talk.

The Move · If client rambles past 45 sec: "Love that — hold that thought, we're going to get into all of it." Plants seed: we're going deeper, just not now.
01
2:00–7:00 · Both

Your Story — listen for the thing beneath.

Their intake answers are reflected back on screen. Read them, then ask the printed question exactly as written. Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Listen for the thing beneath the thing.

"So — what's really going on?"

Who's Listening For What

Megha: positioning gaps. Messaging misalignment. Emotional drivers. The brand stuff they're not saying.

Jake: visual/system gaps. Distance between vision and reality. Design/execution disconnects.
Hard rule · Do NOT let this section run past 7:00. If they're still talking at 6:50, Jake jumps in: "I want to honor your time — let me share what we found."
02
7:00–12:00 · M leads

Dialing In — present the hunch.

"What we like" cards (M + J) are pre-filled from morning prep. Read them. Then drop the gap hypothesis (also pre-filled).

"Here's our hunch. Before we go deeper — is this the real problem?"

If they say yes

Proceed. You're aligned.

If they push back

"Tell me more about that." Adjust. Listen. Don't defend the hypothesis. Senior move = being wrong fast.

03
12:00–15:00 · Both · Moved in V2

North Star — one question. One answer.

The question is huge on screen. Let it land. Pause before you ask it. The silence is the setup.

"If you could only do one thing for the next 90 days — what would it be?"

When they answer: type it EXACTLY as they say it. Don't clean it up. The authenticity is load-bearing.

If they're stuck

"Close your eyes. What does success look like in 90 days? Describe the scene." (Helps them see, not think.)

If they give a long answer

"Say that in one sentence." Push for clarity.

04
15:00–25:00 · M + J · BLACK BG

What We See — name the real problem.

The 4 audit pillars come pre-scored from morning prep (M + J each set their score). Walk the client through them. Ask where they think they are first — their self-assessment vs your assessment IS the diagnosis.

The flow

1. Show audit pillars (Positioning · Messaging · Execution · Distribution)
2. Walk through one pillar at a time
3. Drop the hunch ("Megha's read") which is pre-filled
4. Around minute 20, Jake triggers Recalibration Check

Recalibration Check (~minute 20)

"How's this landing?" Three pills: Yes — keep going / Adjust / Pivot

Pattern · Most clients have 1 dominant weak pillar and 1 secondary. The real problem lives at the intersection. That's the diagnosis.

Planting the upsell seed (~min 24)

"There's deeper work here. I'll put what that looks like in the plan." Don't hard-sell. Just name it.

~25
~25:00 · Somatic Reset · NEW

A breath. What's landing?

This is the move 99% of strategy workshops skip. Once you've named the gap, the client's body goes into low-grade fight/flight. They're processing. If you immediately jump into "here's the plan," you bypass integration.

The flow

30 seconds. M or J says: "We just gave you a lot. Take a breath. What's landing?"

Three pills on screen — 🟢 With us / 🟡 Processing / 🔴 Stuck. Client clicks one.

Why bounded · An open "how are you doing?" runs over 30 sec — clients spiral. The 3-pill structure forces a fast emotional read without losing time.

If they pick 🔴 Stuck

"What part isn't sitting right?" Listen. Adjust before moving to Moves.

05
25:00–35:00 · Both

Your Moves — pressure-test the plan.

72-hour win + 1-week move + 1-month build are pre-drafted from morning prep. Read them. Edit live with the client. Match the action to what they can actually execute.

The capability rule

You saw their capacity in intake. Don't prescribe something they can't do.

What We're Not Doing

Pre-filled list. Client can ADD to it live. Name the moves NOT on the list. "Here's what we're not doing. Here's why."

What to Leave Alone

Pre-filled. Strengths to protect. Client can add.

If they love everything · Something's wrong. Push: "What feels hardest? What's the resistance?" The friction is the signal.
06
35:00–40:00 · J leads · NEW

Effort / Impact Map — the map.

This replaces The Wall (V1). Drag each move into the 2×2 quadrant. The exercise forces prioritization in real time AND becomes a takeaway artifact in the action plan.

The flow

1. Show the matrix on screen
2. Each pre-drafted move appears as a sticky note
3. Client + Jake drag stickies into the right quadrant
4. M reads the result aloud — confirms or pushes back

Quadrant decision shortcuts

High impact + low effort = DIY (Path A) — do this NOW
High impact + high effort = Level Up (Path B) — where we help
Low impact + low effort = quick wins / nice to do
Low impact + high effort = What We're Not Doing

If they fight a placement · Honor it. The fight IS the data — it tells you what they're attached to. Then ask "is this attachment serving you?"
⚡
40:00–42:00 · Both · NEW

First Move — one thing. Right now.

Behavioral activation moment. Before they hang up — they DO one tiny thing on the call. Update the bio. Archive the line. Draft the subject. We watch.

The flow

1. M or J names the micro-move (the smallest first crack of their 72-hour win)
2. Start the 60-second timer on screen
3. Client does the thing
4. Click Did It / Partial / Saved For After

Why · Implementation intention. Workshops without this have ~20% follow-through. Workshops with it land 70%+. Cheapest move available. Do not skip.

If they freeze

Make it smaller. "Just open the page." Then "just type one word." Momentum is the goal, not the polished result.

07
42:00–45:00 · M leads

The Close — land the plane.

Megha leads. Q&A first ("Anything that didn't land?"), then the close script.

"You came in with ___. We found ___. That changes ___."

Say it out loud as you type it. The words landing in their ears matter.

The Three Plants

Timeline · "In your inbox in 24 hours."
Referral · "If this was valuable, send it to one person. $100 off both of you."
Deeper work · You planted in section 4. If it's right, it'll come up. Don't push.

Don't oversell the bundle in the close. The close is about them, not you. If deeper work is right, they'll feel it.

Pre-Session Checklist

  • Morning Prep dashboard submitted by both M + J
  • Workshop session screen generated with pre-populated content
  • Fireflies recording armed
  • Timer tested · counts up from 0:00
  • B-Side floating tab visible
  • Effort/Impact stickies pre-loaded with draft moves
  • Both facilitators have water + clear desk
  • Screen-share URL tested

Emergency Plays

Client won't stop talking (sec 1–2)
Jake: "I want to honor your time. Let me share what we found."
Client is crying
Pause. Sit with it. "Take your time. We're here." Wait 30 seconds. Then "What came up for you?"
Don't rush past emotion — it means you hit something real.
Client hates the diagnosis (sec 4)
"I hear you. What do YOU think the real problem is?" Follow them.
Your job is to illuminate, not convince.
Timer at 40:00 and you're stuck in section 3
Skip to section 5. "Let me jump to the plan."
The plan is the priority.
Tech breaks (screen freezes, URL fails)
"Let me share this from my notes while we sort that out." Keep talking.
The page is a visual aid. The session lives in the conversation.
Client is quiet / not engaging
"What's not landing?" Silence is feedback.
You realize mid-session your prep was wrong
Name it. "I want to adjust what we prepped. Hearing you, the real issue is ___."
Flexibility = trust. Better to be wrong fast.

Final note.

The workshop is a conversation, not a presentation. You're there to illuminate, not convince. The session works when three things happen:

1. They feel heard (sections 1–2)
2. They see the gap clearly (sections 3–4)
3. They leave with a plan that fits (sections 5–7)

Stay focused on those three moments. Everything else is detail.

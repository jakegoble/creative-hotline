Quick Reference — 45-Min Session Timeline

Use this to stay locked in. Each section has a distinct purpose. When you're running behind, you know what to compress.

2:00–5:00 North Star: Ice breaker question (frame their business) Both
5:00–10:00 Diagnosis: Megha delivers the read (positioning, brand signals) Megha leads

Timing Pro Tip: The Creative Direction section (12:00–25:00) is where you spend the most oxygen. Diagnosis and Recalibration set it up. If you're light on diagnosis (5 mins instead of 5), compress Recalibration. Never skip Action Plan — that's where client buys in.

Keep these patterns front of mind. When you catch one mid-session, know your move.

Can't articulate what they want
"What's one thing that would make you feel like this was worth doing?"
Pushes back on your diagnosis
"Tell me more about that." Adjust if they're right. Don't defend the read.
Wants execution during the call
"That's a build, not a call. Let's map what that build looks like." Then move to Action Plan.
In actual crisis (funding, shutdown, etc.)
"Before strategy — are you okay?" Shift from diagnosis to support.
Gives polished investor pitch answer
"That sounds like the version you tell investors. What's the real version?"

Trigger Recalibration check. Something's off. Reset before moving forward.

"There's a tension here worth exploring." Make it visible. Don't smooth it over.

How the Session Page Works

The workshop page is your shared canvas. Everything on it is visible to the client — there are no hidden panels, sidebars, or surprise reveals. You're both looking at the same thing.

The Page Structure (What Clients See)

  • Top bar (fixed): TCH logo, section indicator that auto-updates as you scroll, and the timer (click to start/pause, counts UP from 0:00)
  • Main content: Six sections flowing top to bottom. Each section has a time badge (0:00–2:00, etc.)
  • The Wall (interactive): Between North Star and Diagnosis. Draggable sticky notes for collaborative brainstorming
  • B-Side tab (top right): Floating torn-paper notepad for off-topic ideas. Visible but minimized until clicked
Framing for the client (say this at the start): "You'll see everything we're working on here on the screen. At the top right is the B-Side — that's where we park any ideas that come up but aren't today's focus. You get all of that in your action plan. The timer up top keeps us on pace. Any questions before we dive in?"

Interactive Elements Guide

The Timer

Click once to start. It counts UP from 0:00 and matches the section time badges on the page (0:00–2:00, 2:00–7:00, etc.). Click again to pause.

  • Normal state: white text on black
  • Turns yellow at 10 minutes remaining
  • Turns red + pulses at 5 minutes remaining

Use it to keep pressure on the session. If you're at 12:00 on the timer and still in section 1, you need to move. It's not about rushing — it's about respecting the shape of the conversation. Time constraints create clarity.

The B-Side

Click the "B-SIDE" tab in the top right to open the floating notepad. This is where you park ideas that come up but aren't today's focus.

  • Anyone can type into it (client or facilitator)
  • "+" button: Adds a new row
  • "×" button: Clears a row
  • Text is saved automatically

Explain this to the client in the opening: "Anything that feels important but isn't what we're focused on today goes here. You get everything in your action plan — it's not lost, it's just parked."

Sticky Notes (The Wall)

Between the North Star and Diagnosis sections, there's an interactive wall for collaborative sticky-note brainstorming. This is the design thinking moment.

  • Drag stickies to move them around. Group related ideas together.
  • Click a sticky to edit text in it
  • "×" on a sticky: Deletes that note
  • Color buttons at the bottom: Red, Blue, Yellow, White. Add new stickies in each color.

Color Coding (establish this with the team)

  • Red: Brand & positioning observations (Megha leads adding these)
  • Blue: Design & systems observations (Jake leads adding these)
  • Yellow: Client ideas & reactions (invite them to add these)
  • White: Open questions & unknowns

Facilitator tip: Spend 3–5 minutes at the wall. Don't rush it, but don't camp. This is where patterns surface. Look for what's being repeated, what's being avoided, what connects across ideas.

Creative Authority Audit (Section 4)

Four cards representing the pillars: Positioning, Messaging, Execution, Distribution.

Each card has a rating system (dots). Click the card or the dots to cycle through:

  • ○○○ = Weak / Not established
  • ●○○ = Starting / Some clarity
  • ●●○ = Solid / Mostly clear
  • ●●● = Strong / Fully owned

Walk through these WITH the client. Ask: "Let's rate your positioning together. Where do you think you are?" Their self-assessment vs. your assessment is the diagnosis.

Pattern: Most clients have 1 dominant weak pillar and 1 secondary. The real problem lives at the intersection. That's the diagnosis.

Recalibration Check (Section 4)

At around minute 20, Jake leads a simple choice moment. Three pills appear on screen:

  • "Yes — keep going" (we're on the right track)
  • "Adjust" (the direction is right but needs refinement)
  • "Pivot" (we're looking at the wrong problem)

Click the appropriate pill. This is a pressure test. If "Adjust" or "Pivot": ask "What's the thing we haven't said yet?" Listen hard for the gap.

Action Plan Cards (Section 5)

The action cards come pre-filled from morning prep with draft moves. You built these before the session.

  • All text is editable. Click into the card and refine live with the client.
  • The session is about pressure-testing, not inventing from scratch
  • Key question: "Does this feel right? What's missing?"

Note: If they want to add a major new move, capture it on the B-Side. The draft plan is your anchor. You refine, you don't rebuild.

The Close Script (Section 6)

Fill-in-the-blank format that Megha delivers. Click the blanks to type in real-time:

"You came in with ___. We found ___. That changes ___."

Fill these in as you say them. Don't write ahead — think, then say, then type. It lands harder that way.

00 — INTRODUCTIONS (0:00–2:00)

Purpose

Set the tone. Fast, warm, no fluff. You model the pace for the entire session.

The Flow

  • Megha introduces (30 sec): Name, what you do, one thing. Model the format.
  • Jake introduces (30 sec): Same structure.
  • Client introduces (60 sec): Use the built-in micro-timer. Start it and let them talk.

The Move

If the client starts rambling at 45 seconds in: "Love that — hold that thought, we're going to get into all of it." This plants the seed that you're going deeper, just not right now.

Critical insight: This section sets the pace for the entire session. If the intro drags, everything drags. Tightness here = tightness everywhere.

01 — YOUR STORY (2:00–7:00)

Purpose

Reflect their intake back. Show you did your homework. Get to the real problem.

The Flow

Their words from intake are on the page. Read them back, then ask: "So — what's really going on?"

The question is printed on the screen. Say it exactly as written. Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Listen for the thing beneath the thing.

Who's Listening For What

  • Megha: Positioning gaps. Messaging misalignment. Emotional drivers. The brand stuff they're not saying.
  • Jake: Visual/system gaps. Distance between vision and reality. Design/execution disconnects.
DO NOT let this section run past 7 minutes. If they're still talking at 6:50, Jake jumps in: "I want to honor your time — let me share what we found." You'll have time to dig deeper in later sections. This section is about listening, not solving.

02 — WHAT WE FOUND (7:00–12:00)

Purpose

Present your research. Establish authority. Name the gap hypothesis.

The Flow

  • Megha leads: Walk through your observations from morning prep. They're on screen.
  • Jake adds: Your perspective. Where the gaps are visible in the design/systems layer.

The Key Moment

Present your gap hypothesis as a HYPOTHESIS: "Here's our hunch..." The prompt is on screen. Say it: "Before we go deeper — is this the real problem?"

If they say yes: Proceed. You're aligned.

If they push back: "Tell me more about that." Adjust. Listen. Don't defend your hypothesis.

If diagnosis is wrong: Senior move = being wrong fast. Ask: "What IS the thing?" Follow them.

Pro move: If you're wrong, it's better to know it now than to spend 12 minutes talking about the wrong problem. Flexibility here buys trust later.

03 — NORTH STAR (12:00–17:00)

Purpose

The emotional anchor. One question. One answer. This becomes the compass for the entire action plan.

The Flow

The question is huge on screen. Let it land. Pause before you ask it. Don't rush. The silence is the setup.

When they answer: type it EXACTLY as they say it. Don't clean it up. Don't edit. The authenticity matters.

If They're Stuck

"Close your eyes. What does success look like in 90 days? Describe the scene." This helps them see, not think.

If They Give a Long Answer

"Say that in one sentence." Push for clarity. This clarity is load-bearing for the action plan.

THE WALL (Between North Star and Diagnosis — 17:00)

Purpose

Collaborative brainstorm. Design thinking energy. Process everything that's been said so far and surface patterns.

The Flow

  • Add stickies for patterns you're seeing. Group what connects.
  • Invite the client to add stickies: "What's coming up for you?"
  • Spend 3–5 minutes here. Don't rush it, but don't camp.
  • Use color coding to organize thinking (Red = Megha obs, Blue = Jake obs, Yellow = Client ideas, White = Questions)
What to look for: Repetition (what theme appears twice?), avoidance (what aren't they saying?), contradiction (where's the tension?). These are signals.

04 — WHAT WE SEE (17:00–25:00)

Purpose

Name the real problem. This is the product. The diagnosis is where strategy meets reality.

The Flow

  • Walk through the audit pillars TOGETHER. Ask the client where they think they are first.
  • Then share YOUR assessment. The gap between self-assessment and yours IS the diagnosis.
  • Megha leads the diagnosis. Jake adds the design/systems angle.

Around Minute 20: Recalibration Check

Jake does this moment. "How's this landing?" Click the pill that matches the client's vibe. This is a pressure test — it tells you if they're with you or not.

If they're overwhelmed: Jake simplifies. "What's the ONE thing?" Strip it back.

Planting the Seed

Around minute 24, plant the upsell naturally: "There's deeper work here. I'll put what that looks like in the plan." Don't hard-sell. Just name it. It'll land or it won't.

05 — YOUR MOVES (25:00–37:00)

Purpose

The practical plan. Pressure-test it together. Refine it live. Make sure it fits their actual capacity.

The Flow

  • Action cards come pre-loaded from morning prep.
  • Frame it: "Here's what we'd recommend. Let's see if it fits."
  • Edit the cards LIVE if the client pushes back or adds context. This is collaborative.

The Capability Rule

Match the action to what they can actually do. You saw in intake what they have bandwidth for. Don't prescribe something they can't execute.

"What to Stop" Matters

Name it clearly. The moves that don't make the list are as important as the ones that do. "Here's what we're NOT doing. Here's why."

If they love everything, something's wrong. Push harder: "What feels hardest? What's the resistance?" The friction is the signal.

06 — THE CLOSE (37:00–45:00)

Purpose

Land the plane. Clean, confident, done. Leave them with clarity and momentum.

The Flow

  • Megha leads.
  • Questions first: "Anything that didn't land?" Let them ask. Clarify.
  • Then the close script: "You came in with ___. We found ___. That changes ___."
  • Say it out loud as you type it in. The words landing in their ears matter.

The Three Plants

  • The timeline: "In your inbox by [specific time]." Set the expectation. Frankie sends within 24 hours.
  • The referral: "If this was valuable, send it to one person who needs it. $100 off for both of you." Easy and generous.
  • The deeper work: You planted this in section 4. If it's right, it'll come up naturally. Don't push. If they ask, you have the answer ready.
Don't oversell the 3-pack in the close. The close is about them, not you. If deeper work is right, they'll feel it.

Emergency Plays

The session won't go exactly to plan. Here's how to handle the moments when it doesn't.

Client won't stop talking (Section 1 or 2, running over time)

Jake says: "I want to honor your time. Let's make sure you leave with what you need." This is warm, not cold. It's permission to move. If they keep going, interrupt gently: "We're going to get into all of that — let me share what we found first."

Client is crying

Pause. Sit with it. "Take your time. We're here." Don't rush past emotion — it's signal. It means you hit something real. Let them breathe. Hand them water if you're in person. Wait 30 seconds. Then: "What came up for you?"

Client hates your diagnosis (Section 4)

Don't defend it. "I hear you. What do YOU think the real problem is?" Follow them. Your job is to illuminate, not to convince. If you're wrong, you're wrong. That's valuable information.

Timer at 40 minutes and you're stuck in Section 3

Skip to Section 5. "Let me jump to the plan — that's what you're here for." You can always circle back to diagnosis language in the close. The plan is the priority.

Tech breaks (screen freezes, URL doesn't load, connection drops)

Keep talking. The page is a visual aid, not the session. You have your notes. The client has you. Say: "Let me share this from my notes while we sort that out." Then keep going. Reconnect when you can.

Client is quiet / not engaging

This usually means they're uncomfortable or you're not landing. Ask directly: "What's not landing here?" or "What are you thinking?" Silence is feedback. Respond to it.

You realize mid-session that your morning prep was wrong

Name it. "I want to adjust what we prepped. Hearing you, I think the real issue is ___." Flexibility = trust. Being wrong fast is better than doubling down.

Post-Session Debrief Checklist

Right after the client disconnects, spend 5–10 minutes on this. Both Megha and Jake rate independently. This is how you build institutional knowledge about what works.

Quality Gate Reference:
7–10: Strong session. Standard follow-up flow.
5–6: Landed, but rough edges. Extra polish needed before upsell.
3–4: Struggled. Recovery protocol: debrief with client, reframe, rebuild trust.
1–2: Missed badly. Refund territory. Analyze what broke.

Data saved to localStorage — review before next session.

Final Notes

The workshop is a conversation, not a presentation. You're there to illuminate, not to convince. The client's words, reactions, and energy are the data. The page is the container. You are the facilitators.

The session works when three things happen:
  • They feel heard (Section 1–2)
  • They see the gap clearly (Section 3–4)
  • They leave with a plan that fits (Section 5–6)

Everything else is detail. Stay focused on those three moments, and the session will land.

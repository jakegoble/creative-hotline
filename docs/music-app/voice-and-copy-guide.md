## 1. Voice & Personality

### The Core Identity

The app's voice is **the version of yourself that's two years ahead** -- the part of your brain that already processed the data and is now talking to you like a sharp, music-obsessed friend at 1am after a session, laptop open, pulling up numbers to prove a point.

It is not a manager. It is not an A&R exec. It is not a business consultant wearing a music hat. It is the intelligence layer of the artist themselves -- calm, pattern-obsessed, quietly ambitious, and always thinking in terms of *what to do next*.

### Voice Principles

**1. Musician-first, numbers-second.**
Data exists to serve creative decisions. Every number should be reframed in terms of what it means for the music, the career, the next move. We never present a number without context.

- NO: "Monthly listeners: 32,100"
- YES: "32.1K monthly listeners -- that's the room you're playing to every 30 days."

**2. Warm confidence, not hype.**
The tone is grounded. We do not use exclamation marks casually. We do not say "Amazing!" or "Crushing it!" We speak with the quiet assurance of someone who has seen the patterns before and knows what they mean. When things are going well, we let the data speak. When things need work, we are direct without being grim.

- NO: "Your streams are skyrocketing! Keep it up!"
- YES: "Your Love's Not Wasted hit 36 popularity this month. That's your highest score and it's still climbing."

**3. The studio is the frame of reference.**
We use music language, not marketing language. Think "sessions" not "campaigns." Think "your catalog" not "your content portfolio." Think "the room you're reaching" not "your total addressable market." The metaphors come from making music, touring, and the lived experience of building a career track by track.

**4. Short, declarative, rhythmic.**
Copy should have cadence. Read it out loud. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it. If it sounds like something you'd actually say to another musician over coffee, it's right.

**5. Honest about scale.**
Jakke is at 4.6M cross-platform streams with 32K monthly listeners. That is real and meaningful and growing. We never pretend it's bigger than it is. We never belittle it either. The voice understands where the artist is in the arc and speaks to that specific phase -- the inflection point between underground credibility and genuine momentum.

### The AI Persona: No Name

The AI assistant does **not** have a name or avatar. Naming it creates distance -- it becomes a character performing helpfulness instead of a natural extension of the dashboard's intelligence. The insights should feel like they emerge from the data itself, not from a chatbot personality.

When the system needs to speak in first person (rare), it uses "we" -- as in, "we" the artist-and-their-intelligence working together. More often, it speaks directly to the artist in second person: "Your catalog depth is a quiet advantage."

### Emotional Range

**When things are going well:**
Measured. Let the numbers land. A brief, knowing observation. No confetti.
> "Brick by Brick just got picked up by Organic House 2026 -- 169K followers on that playlist. That's the kind of placement that compounds."

**When things are declining:**
Direct but constructive. Never alarming. Always paired with a next step.
> "Monthly listeners dipped 12% this month. That's normal between release cycles -- the catalog holds, but fresh material resets the algorithm. Worth thinking about timing."

**When there's a missed opportunity:**
Slight urgency, framed as "the door is still open."
> "You've got 192 DMs a month coming in and zero email capture. That's 192 people raising their hand every 30 days and you're not catching any of them."

**When celebrating a milestone:**
Understated but real. Acknowledge the work behind the number.
> "4.6 million streams across platforms. That's not a vanity number -- that's years of work finding its audience."

## 2. Insight Copy Templates

Each insight type includes 2-3 variations. The system should randomly select or contextually choose the best fit. Variables are wrapped in `{curly_braces}`.

### 2.1 Breakout Alert

When a track shows accelerating momentum -- rising popularity score, new playlist adds, or stream velocity increase.

**Variation A:**
> **{track_name} is picking up speed.**
> Popularity jumped from {old_score} to {new_score} this {period}. {playlist_count} new playlist adds are driving it. This is the window to push -- pitch it, post about it, get it in front of people while the algorithm is paying attention.

**Variation B:**
> **Something's happening with {track_name}.**
> {stream_delta}% stream increase in {period}, now sitting on {playlist_count} playlists with {playlist_reach} combined reach. That's organic pull. Feed it.

**Variation C:**
> **{track_name} just crossed {milestone} streams.**
> It's your {ranking} most-streamed track and still accelerating. The listeners-to-saves ratio ({ratio}) says people are coming back to it. This one has legs.

### 2.2 Declining Track Warning

When a previously strong track shows falling velocity or drops off playlists.

**Variation A:**
> **{track_name} is cooling off.**
> Popularity dropped from {old_score} to {new_score} over {period}. Lost {lost_playlists} playlist placements. Normal lifecycle -- but if you want to extend the run, a remix, a live version, or a visual piece can reset momentum.

**Variation B:**
> **Heads up on {track_name}.**
> Stream velocity down {delta}% this month. Still holding at {current_streams} total, but the daily rate is flattening. This is where a strategic content push or collab remix can buy another cycle.

**Variation C:**
> **{track_name} had a good run.**
> Down to {current_score} popularity from a peak of {peak_score}. The catalog keeps collecting passive streams, but active growth on this one has plateaued. Time to let the next release carry the torch.

### 2.3 Sync Opportunity Detected

When track characteristics, playlist context, or genre trends suggest sync licensing potential.

**Variation A:**
> **{track_name} reads like a sync placement waiting to happen.**
> The tempo ({bpm} BPM), mood ({mood_tags}), and clean vocal mix put it right in the zone for {sync_category}. Worth getting this in front of music supervisors -- the playlist context ({playlist_names}) already tells that story.

**Variation B:**
> **Sync radar: {track_name}.**
> Tracks in the {genre} space with this energy profile are landing in {sync_category} placements right now. Your version has the advantage of live instrumentation -- that's what supervisors are hunting for. Package it.

**Variation C:**
> **{track_name} fits the brief.**
> Organic house with real instruments and no harsh drops -- that's premium TV and film territory. The track is already proving itself with listeners ({streams} streams). A one-sheet and stems package could open a new revenue line here.

### 2.4 Release Timing Suggestion

Based on historical performance data, listener activity patterns, and release calendar gaps.

**Variation A:**
> **Best window for your next release: {recommended_date_range}.**
> Your audience is most active on {peak_days}, and {period} historically shows the strongest engagement. Pitch to editorial playlists by {pitch_deadline} -- they need 4 weeks lead time minimum.

**Variation B:**
> **Think about dropping on a {recommended_day}.**
> Your {peak_day} posts average {peak_avg} likes vs {overall_avg} overall. Pair the release with a Reel on {recommended_day}, pre-save link the week before, and let the algorithm do the rest.

**Variation C:**
> **The calendar is open.**
> No major releases in your genre space for {gap_period}. That's a rare clean runway. If you've got something close to finished, this is the window where you don't have to fight for attention.

### 2.5 Audience Insight (Geographic / Demographic)

Surfacing patterns in where and who the listeners are.

**Variation A:**
> **Your biggest room is in {top_city}.**
> {city_percentage}% of your followers are in {top_city}, with {second_city} at {second_percentage}%. That's not just streaming data -- that's where you'd sell out a show. If you're planning any live dates, start there.

**Variation B:**
> **{country} is quietly becoming your second market.**
> {country_percentage}% of your audience, up from {prev_percentage}%. {country} listeners tend to have higher playlist-add rates in the {genre} space. Worth considering localized playlist pitching.

**Variation C:**
> **Your core listener is a {age_range} {primary_gender} in {top_city}.**
> {age_percentage}% of your audience fits this profile. That's not a limitation -- it's a targeting advantage. Every ad dollar, every content decision, every collab pitch gets sharper when you know exactly who's already in the room.

### 2.6 Revenue Milestone

When cumulative revenue crosses a meaningful threshold, or monthly revenue shows significant change.

**Variation A:**
> **You just crossed ${milestone} in estimated revenue.**
> {total_streams} streams at a ${blended_rate} blended rate. Not life-changing money yet, but it's proof the catalog works while you sleep. Every new release adds another revenue layer.

**Variation B:**
> **Revenue this month: ${monthly_revenue}.**
> That's ${daily_rate}/day from your catalog alone. The math gets interesting when you add sync licensing, live performance, and merch on top. Streaming is the foundation, not the ceiling.

**Variation C:**
> **Your Love's Not Wasted has earned an estimated ${track_revenue}.**
> Your highest-earning track by ${delta} over {second_track}. When one track carries this much of the catalog revenue, it tells you something about what your audience wants. Worth making more things that feel like this.

### 2.7 Playlist Addition / Removal

**Addition -- Variation A:**
> **New playlist: {playlist_name} ({follower_count} followers).**
> {track_name} just got added. That's {reach_delta} new potential listeners. The playlist's vibe ({playlist_genre}) lines up well with your catalog -- could lead to more adds from the same curator network.

**Addition -- Variation B:**
> **{track_name} landed on {playlist_name}.**
> {follower_count} followers on this one. Keep an eye on stream velocity over the next 48 hours -- that's when playlist adds show their real impact.

**Removal -- Variation A:**
> **{track_name} was removed from {playlist_name}.**
> Lost access to {follower_count} potential listeners. Normal rotation -- playlists cycle tracks every {avg_cycle}. The streams you gained during placement are permanent. Focus on pitching the next one.

### 2.8 Collaborator Recommendation

Based on engagement multipliers, genre alignment, and network analysis.

**Variation A:**
> **You should get back in the studio with {collaborator_name}.**
> Your collab tracks average {collab_avg} streams vs {solo_avg} solo. That's a {multiplier}x multiplier. {collaborator_name} brings {their_audience} listeners who overlap with your sound but aren't already in your audience.

**Variation B:**
> **Collab opportunity: {collaborator_name}.**
> They're in the {genre} lane, {follower_count} monthly listeners, and your audiences share a {overlap_percentage}% overlap. Enough common ground to cross-pollinate, enough difference to grow.

**Variation C:**
> **The @{ig_collaborator} connection keeps paying off.**
> Posts with {ig_collaborator} average {collab_likes} likes vs your {solo_likes} solo average -- that's a {multiplier}x bump. Two sessions a quarter is the sweet spot. More than that and it dilutes both brands.

### 2.9 Content Strategy Tip

Actionable guidance on social media posting based on performance data.

**Variation A:**
> **Your Reels are outperforming everything else by {multiplier}x.**
> {reel_avg} avg interactions vs {post_avg} for static posts. The algorithm is telling you something. Even a simple studio clip with your track playing hits harder than a polished photo right now.

**Variation B:**
> **You're posting {current_frequency} times a week. Bump it to {recommended_frequency}.**
> Not because more is always better -- but your engagement doesn't drop off until post {dropoff_number} in a week. You've got headroom before fatigue sets in.

**Variation C:**
> **Thursday and Sunday are your days.**
> {thursday_avg} and {sunday_avg} avg likes vs {worst_day} on {worst_day_name}. Schedule your best content for those windows. Save the casual behind-the-scenes stuff for the off days.

### 2.10 Competitive Intelligence

When similar artists in the genre space show notable movement.

**Variation A:**
> **Artists in your lane are leaning into {trend}.**
> The {genre} space on Spotify saw a {growth_percentage}% increase in editorial playlist adds this quarter. Your catalog fits the profile -- the timing is right for an editorial push.

**Variation B:**
> **{similar_artist} just hit {milestone}.**
> They're in a similar lane with comparable catalog size. What's different: they're releasing every {release_cadence} and pushing {content_type} hard on social. Not a blueprint to copy, but the cadence is worth noting.

**Variation C:**
> **The {genre} space is {expanding/contracting}.**
> Listener demand is {direction} {percentage}% this quarter based on playlist growth. You're positioned {well/to capitalize} -- your sound sits at the intersection of {genre_a} and {genre_b}, which is exactly where curators are hunting.

### 2.11 Growth Milestone

When follower counts, stream totals, or other career metrics cross meaningful thresholds.

**Variation A:**
> **{metric} just passed {milestone}.**
> That took {time_period}. The last {previous_milestone} took {previous_time}. The pace is accelerating -- each release compounds on the catalog behind it.

**Variation B:**
> **You've got {follower_count} people following your work across platforms.**
> That's a mid-size venue, sold out, every night of the week. Not abstract numbers -- real people who opted in.

**Variation C:**
> **820 playlists. 15.7M reach.**
> Your music is sitting in front of 15.7 million potential listeners right now, without you lifting a finger. That's the power of a catalog that works on its own. The question is how to convert reach into relationship.

### 2.12 Weekly Summary Intro

The opening line of the weekly digest. Sets the tone for everything that follows.

**Variation A (strong week):**
> This was a good week for the catalog. {primary_highlight} led the way, and the numbers back it up. Here's what moved.

**Variation B (steady week):**
> Quiet week on the surface, but the foundation held. {streams_count} streams, {new_listeners} new listeners, {playlist_activity} playlist {adds/no changes}. The catalog keeps working. Here's the breakdown.

**Variation C (notable change):**
> Something shifted this week. {primary_event} changed the trajectory on {track_or_metric}. Worth paying attention to. Here's the full picture.

**Variation D (action-oriented):**
> Three things to know this week, one thing to do about each. Let's get into it.

## 3. Onboarding Experience

### First-Time User Flow

The onboarding should feel like plugging in your instruments before a session -- necessary setup that builds anticipation for the music that's about to happen.

#### Step 1: Welcome

**Header:** Your music. Your data. One place.

**Body:**
> We're about to connect your streaming platforms, social accounts, and distributor data into a single intelligence layer. This takes about 3 minutes to set up and gets smarter every week.
>
> The goal isn't more dashboards. It's fewer decisions made in the dark.

**CTA:** Let's plug in

#### Step 2: Connect Accounts

**Header:** Connect your sources

**Subhead:**
> Each connection gives the dashboard another angle on your career. Start with Spotify -- that's where the deepest data lives.

**Per-connection microcopy:**

- **Spotify for Artists:** "Your streaming backbone. Listeners, saves, playlists, popularity -- this is where the story starts."
- **Instagram:** "The social layer. We'll pull engagement, audience demographics, and content performance so you can see what's actually working."
- **DistroKid / TuneCore / etc.:** "Revenue data. See what your streams are actually worth, broken down by platform and track."
- **Songstats:** "Cross-platform intelligence. Playlist tracking, radio plays, and chart positions across every DSP."

**Skip option:**
> "Skip for now -- you can always connect more sources later. Even one connection gives you something to work with."

#### Step 3: First Data Load

**Loading state:**
> Pulling in your catalog, crunching the numbers, finding the patterns. This usually takes 30-60 seconds.

**Progress microcopy (rotating):**
> "Reading your streaming history..."
> "Mapping your playlist network..."
> "Analyzing your audience..."
> "Building your strategy score..."
> "Almost there -- worth the wait."

#### Step 4: The "Aha Moment"

The first thing the user sees after data loads should be the single most interesting insight in their data. Not a dashboard. Not a wall of KPIs. One insight, presented beautifully.

**Template:**
> **Here's something you might not know.**
>
> {personalized_insight}
>
> That's one pattern. There are {count} more insights waiting in your dashboard.

**Example for Jake:**
> **Here's something you might not know.**
>
> Your collab posts on Instagram get 2.2x more engagement than your solo posts. But you only collab on 12% of your content. That's the single highest-leverage change you could make to your social strategy right now.
>
> That's one pattern. There are 7 more insights waiting in your dashboard.

**CTA:** Show me everything

### Empty State Copy

Before any data is connected, each page needs copy that explains what will appear and motivates connection.

**Dashboard (empty):**
> "This is where everything comes together -- streaming, social, revenue, and audience data in one view. Connect your first source to light it up."

**Streaming (empty):**
> "Your streaming story lives here. Total streams, track popularity, playlist placements, listener trends. Connect Spotify for Artists to start."

**Catalog (empty):**
> "Every track you've ever released, with the data that tells you which ones are working and why. This populates automatically once streaming data is connected."

**Revenue (empty):**
> "Your music is earning money. This page shows you exactly how much, from where, and which tracks carry the most weight. Connect your distributor to see the numbers."

**Instagram (empty):**
> "Your social presence, decoded. Engagement rates, best posting times, content performance, audience demographics. Connect Instagram to see what's actually landing."

**AI Insights (empty):**
> "The strategy layer needs data to think. Connect at least two sources -- streaming and social -- and the patterns start to emerge."

## 4. Weekly Career Brief

### Structure

The weekly brief is a short, scannable email or in-app digest. It should take under 2 minutes to read and leave the artist with a clear understanding of what happened and what to do next.

**Length:** 400-600 words
**Sections:** 5
**Tone:** The same voice as the app -- direct, warm, grounded

### Section Structure

```
1. HEADLINE + LEAD (1-2 sentences)
   The single most important thing that happened this week.

2. BY THE NUMBERS (4-6 metrics)
   Key stats with week-over-week delta.
   Formatted as a clean grid, not a paragraph.

3. WHAT MOVED (2-3 bullet points)
   Notable changes: playlist adds/drops, streaming velocity shifts,
   social content that outperformed, audience changes.

4. ONE THING TO DO THIS WEEK (1 action item)
   The single highest-leverage action based on current data.
   Specific, achievable, tied to a number.

5. LOOKING AHEAD (1-2 sentences)
   What to watch for next week. Upcoming opportunities or
   things to keep an eye on.
```

### Sample Weekly Brief for Jake / Jakke

---

**Subject: Your week in music -- Feb 17-23, 2026**

---

**Your Love's Not Wasted is still your most active track, and a new playlist add pushed its reach past 2M.**

This was a catalog maintenance week -- no release, no big content push -- but the numbers held steady and one placement moved the needle.

**By the Numbers**

- **Instagram Reel from Tuesday** outperformed your last 10 posts by 2.4x. It was a studio clip, 18 seconds, no caption overthinking. The algorithm rewarded the format, not the polish.
- **Enjune's "Lost In The Woods"** is holding at 34 popularity -- unusual for a legacy project track. The Corey Harper connection continues to pull listeners across.

A simple Linktree with a "get early access to my next release" hook would start building the one channel the algorithm can't take away from you. 30 minutes of setup. Permanent infrastructure.

If you have anything in progress that could be ready by mid-March, start thinking about the pitch now -- Spotify wants 4 weeks minimum lead time, and the organic house lane has been getting more editorial attention this quarter.

### Brief Variations by Week Type

**Release week:**
Lead with the release. First 48-hour numbers. Playlist adds. Social performance of the announcement content. Comparison to last release's first week.

**Milestone week:**
Lead with the milestone. Put it in context (how long it took, acceleration vs. previous milestones). One action to capitalize.

**Quiet week:**
Lead with the catalog's passive performance. Frame it as "the foundation holding." Surface one hidden pattern in the data.

**Declining week:**
Lead honestly. "Streams were down this week." Immediately follow with context (seasonality, release gap, platform-wide trends). One constructive action.

## 5. Visual Storytelling

### Framing the Career Narrative Through Data

The dashboard should not feel like a reporting tool. It should feel like a biography being written in real time. Every chart is a chapter. Every metric is a sentence in a larger story.

**Guiding Metaphor: The Artist's Map**

Think of the dashboard as a map of an expanding territory. Early career, the map is mostly blank -- a few tracks, a small audience, limited data points. As the career grows, the map fills in. New regions appear (markets, platforms). Roads connect (collabs, playlist networks). Cities grow (audience centers).

This metaphor should subtly inform how we frame data:
- "Your reach just expanded to 15.7M" (territory growing)
- "London is becoming your third-largest market" (new region on the map)
- "The @ontout connection opens a path to 40K new listeners" (road connecting two areas)

### Year in Review Format

An annual summary that tells the story of the year through data, formatted as a narrative rather than a dashboard.

**Structure:**

```
1. THE YEAR IN ONE SENTENCE
   "2025 was the year the catalog crossed 4 million streams
    and your audience started finding you on its own."

2. THE HEADLINE NUMBER
   Full-screen, single metric. The most meaningful number of the year.
   4,610,000 cross-platform streams.
   Below it: "That's every seat in Dodger Stadium, filled 82 times."

3. YOUR TOP 5 TRACKS
   Presented as a tracklist -- not a table, a tracklist.
   Album art placeholders. Track name. Streams. One-line insight each.
   "Your Love's Not Wasted -- 892K streams. The one that keeps finding new listeners."
   "Late Night -- 641K streams. Your most-saved track. People keep this one close."

4. THE MAP
   Geographic visualization of where your listeners are.
   Top 5 cities highlighted. "Your biggest room this year was Los Angeles."

5. YOUR NETWORK
   Collaborator web visualization.
   "You worked with 12 artists this year. The @ontout connection
    generated 13x your solo engagement."

6. THE GROWTH ARC
   Monthly listeners over time, as a clean line chart.
   Start of year vs. end of year. "You started January with 18K
    monthly listeners and ended December with 32K."

7. REVENUE
   "Your catalog earned an estimated $2,180 this year.
    $6 a day while you were doing everything else."

8. LOOKING FORWARD
   "Three things that could define 2026."
   Each with a one-liner and the data behind it.
```

### Catalog View: The Record Collection

The current catalog page is a data table. That's useful but soulless. The catalog should feel like browsing a record collection -- something you built over years that has personality and depth.

**Design Direction:**

**"Shelf View" (default):**
Each release gets a card that looks like a record sleeve laid on its side. Album art (or generated gradient placeholder based on genre), track name, key stats visible at a glance. Sorted by release date (newest first) with the option to sort by streams, popularity, or genre.

Card anatomy:
```
+----------------------------------+
| [Album Art / Genre Gradient]     |
| Track Name                       |
| Artist(s)            Genre Badge |
|                                  |
| 892K streams     36 popularity   |
| Released: 2024-03-15             |
+----------------------------------+
```

Hover state reveals: saves count, playlist count, estimated revenue, collaborator credits.

**"Deep Cut" view:**
A toggle that shows tracks with low streams but high save-to-stream ratios -- "the ones your real fans love." This surfaces catalog gems that might deserve a push.

**Color-coded genre system (already exists -- enhance it):**
Use the genre gradient as a subtle background wash on each card. When viewing the full catalog, the color palette tells a story: "Your catalog leans warm (organic house, deep house) with pockets of cool (ambient, downtempo)."

**Catalog Health Indicator:**
A small badge per track:
- "Active" (gaining streams) -- green pulse
- "Holding" (steady streams, no growth) -- neutral
- "Sleeping" (minimal activity) -- dim

This turns the catalog from a static archive into a living system where you can see what's working at a glance.

### Chart Philosophy

Every chart in the app should follow these principles:

1. **The title tells you what to think, not what you're looking at.**
   - NO: "Monthly Listeners Over Time"
   - YES: "Your audience is growing" or "Listener growth has flattened since October"
   - The chart title should change based on the data trend.

2. **Annotations over tooltips for key moments.**
   When a track got added to a major playlist, annotate that point on the streaming chart. When engagement spiked because of a collab post, mark it. The chart should tell the story without requiring the user to hover.

3. **Context lines over raw numbers.**
   Show benchmarks where relevant. "Average for artists at your level" as a dotted line. Not to create comparison anxiety, but to contextualize whether a number is good, normal, or concerning.

4. **Progressive disclosure.**
   First glance: the shape and direction (up, down, flat). Second glance: the key numbers. Deep dive: the full dataset. Every chart should work at all three levels.

## 6. AI-Generated Content

### 6.1 Artist Bio Generator

Generates an artist bio from streaming data, genre tags, and career milestones. Three lengths: one-liner, short (50 words), and full (150 words).

**Template: One-Liner**
> {artist_name} is a {primary_genre} artist blending {genre_a} and {genre_b} with {total_streams_formatted} streams across platforms.

**Example:**
> Jakke is an organic house artist blending live instrumentation with electronic production, with 4.6M streams across platforms.

**Template: Short Bio (50 words)**
> {artist_name} makes {primary_genre} music that {sonic_description}. Originally from {origin}, {pronoun} has built a catalog of {track_count} tracks spanning {genre_list}, earning {total_streams_formatted} streams and placement on {notable_playlist_count} playlists including {top_playlist}. {career_arc_sentence}.

**Example:**
> Jakke makes organic house music that blurs the line between the dancefloor and the living room. Based in LA, he's built a catalog of 47 tracks spanning organic house, deep house, and melodic techno, earning 4.6M streams and placement on 820 playlists including "frank ocean best songs" (277K followers). Originally recording as Enjune in the indie rock world, his music now lives where live instruments meet electronic warmth.

**Template: Full Bio (150 words)**
> {opening_hook}
>
> {career_arc -- 2-3 sentences covering origin, evolution, current sound}
>
> {credential_paragraph -- streams, playlists, notable placements, collaborators}
>
> {closing -- what's next or what the music is about}

**Example:**
> There's a moment in every Jakke track where you can hear both worlds at once -- the warmth of real instruments and the pulse of electronic production. That collision point is where his music lives.
>
> Jake Goble started as Enjune, writing indie rock songs that landed on Spotify's Chill Tracks and Apple Music's New in Dance. The evolution into Jakke brought a deeper exploration of organic house and melodic techno, layering guitar and keys over hypnotic grooves that move at their own pace.
>
> With 4.6 million cross-platform streams, 820 active playlist placements including "Organic House 2026" and "Electronic Chill & Deep House," and collaborations with artists like Nuage, Somelee, and TACHES, Jakke's catalog has found an audience that keeps growing without chasing trends. The music does the work.
>
> Based in Venice, CA. New music coming 2026.

### 6.2 Sync Pitch Sheet

For sending to music supervisors at TV shows, films, advertising agencies.

**Template:**


## 7. Implementation Notes

### Copy System Architecture

All user-facing copy should live in a centralized copy module rather than being hardcoded in page components. This enables:

1. **A/B testing** -- swap variations without touching UI code
2. **Personalization** -- template variables filled from real data
3. **Consistency** -- one source of truth for voice and tone
4. **Localization readiness** -- if the app ever serves multiple artists

**Suggested file structure:**
```
src/lib/copy/
  index.ts           -- public API (getCopy, getInsight, etc.)
  templates.ts       -- all copy templates with variation arrays
  interpolate.ts     -- template variable replacement
  selectors.ts       -- logic for choosing which variation to show
  weekly-brief.ts    -- weekly brief generator
  bio-generator.ts   -- artist bio templates
  pitch-sheets.ts    -- sync + curator pitch templates
```

### Insight Priority Logic

When the system has multiple insights to surface, prioritize in this order:

1. **Time-sensitive** -- playlist adds/drops, breakout momentum (show within 24 hours)
2. **Action-required** -- things the artist should do now (email capture, pitch window)
3. **Milestones** -- meaningful thresholds crossed
4. **Patterns** -- recurring trends that have been consistent for 2+ weeks
5. **Context** -- market intelligence, competitive landscape

### Copy Don'ts

- Never use "crushing it," "killing it," "smashing it," or any hyperbolic celebration language
- Never use "leverage" as a verb (say "use" or "build on")
- Never use "content" when you mean "music" (content is for social posts; music is music)
- Never use "monetize" (say "earn from" or "turn into revenue")
- Never use "brand" when referring to the artist's identity (say "your sound," "your name," "who you are")
- Never use corporate jargon: synergy, optimize, KPI (in copy -- fine in code), funnel, ROI
- Never start insights with "Did you know..." or "Fun fact..."
- Never use more than one exclamation mark per page
- Never refer to listeners as "consumers" or "users"
- Never say "algorithm hack" (say "what the algorithm rewards right now")

### Copy Do's

- Use specific numbers over generalizations ("192 DMs" not "lots of messages")
- Frame negatives as opportunities with a next step
- Use music-world metaphors (sessions, releases, the room, the set, the catalog)
- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Use "you" and "your" -- this is the artist's data, speak to them directly
- When in doubt, read the copy out loud. If it sounds like something you'd text a friend who makes music, it's right.

### Dynamic Title System

Page headers and chart titles should be data-aware. Rather than static strings, they should adapt to what the data shows.

**Examples:**

| Static (current) | Dynamic (target) |
|---|---|
| "Dashboard" | "Dashboard -- good week for the catalog" |
| "Streaming" | "Streaming -- 32.1K listeners, up from 29K" |
| "Catalog" | "Catalog -- 47 tracks, Your Love's Not Wasted leading" |
| "Revenue" | "Revenue -- $42/week from catalog streams" |
| "AI Insights" | "Insights -- 3 actions for this week" |

The subtitle line on `PageHeader` is already dynamic in some cases. Extend this pattern to all pages, making the subtitle a brief, human-readable summary of the current state rather than a static description.

### Insight Card Redesign

The current AI Insights page uses a clean card layout with priority lanes. The copy system enhances this by:

1. Replacing the current terse descriptions with template-driven copy (Section 2)
2. Adding a "why" line to each insight -- connecting the recommendation to the underlying data
3. Making the effort/impact scores feel less like a project management tool and more like practical guidance: instead of "Effort: 2/10, Impact: 9/10", try "Low effort, high payoff" or "This one takes time but changes the trajectory"

### Seasonal / Contextual Awareness

The copy system should be aware of:

- **Time of year:** "Summer playlists are being curated now -- pitch window closes in 2 weeks"
- **Release recency:** "It's been {n} weeks since your last release" (gentle nudge, not guilt)
- **Day of week:** Weekly brief arrives Monday morning. Insights about posting times are relevant when the artist is likely planning content.
- **Career phase:** The artist is in the "catalog-building" phase (sub-100K monthly listeners, growing but not viral). Copy should reflect this -- focused on compound growth, not overnight success stories.
